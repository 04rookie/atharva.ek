The Problem: Bridging the Dialect Gap

In New York, Caribbean Spanish, particularly the Puerto Rican dialect, is widely spoken. This dialect differs significantly from the Standard Spanish taught in universities, leading to frequent miscommunications in critical environments like healthcare. For nursing and pharmacy students, these communication gaps can have severe implications for patient care. Our project aims to mitigate these challenges by creating a tool for students to learn and practice these specific dialectal variations.

Our Solution: A Real-Time Practice Tool

We developed a real-time, interactive speech-to-speech (S2S) system that allows students to engage in simulated conversations. The tool listens to the user, generates a contextually appropriate response, and speaks it back in a natural-sounding Caribbean Spanish accent. This provides a safe and effective environment for students to practice their conversational skills at their own pace. The core challenge lies in the scarcity of annotated data and models for low-resource dialects. Our approach focuses on fine-tuning existing state-of-the-art models on the limited available data, leveraging the high-performance computing (HPC) resources at the University at Buffalo.

Technical Deep Dive & My Role

As the lead on this project, I was responsible for the end-to-end architecture and implementation. My key contributions include:

  • Data Staging and TTS Fine-Tuning: I built a comprehensive data staging pipeline that included normalization, diarization, forced alignment, and audio chunking. Using this processed data, I fine-tuned the Coqui XTTS-v2 model, successfully mitigating common TTS hallucinations by strategically blending domain-specific and high-quality general corpora.
  • Iterative Model Improvement: I conducted iterative post-training of both the Gemma (LLM) and XTTS (TTS) components. This process, guided by evaluation protocols co-designed with linguistics PhD researchers, significantly improved the conversational naturalness and clinical relevance of the system's responses.
  • Real-Time System Deployment: I deployed the complete S2S system on an OpenStack private cloud, powered by LiveKit for real-time communication. The architecture serves a streaming pipeline (ASR → LLM → fine-tuned XTTS) through authenticated WebRTC sessions, ensuring low-latency interactions for clinical simulation studies.
  • Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration: I partnered closely with clinical faculty to translate ambiguous educational requirements into a scalable and robust speech-to-speech AI architecture, ensuring the final product met the practical needs of its end-users.
